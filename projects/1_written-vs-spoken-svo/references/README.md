# Reference literature

The PDFs themselves are **not tracked in git** (they are third-party copyrighted
papers, ~35 MB). They live locally under `papers/` but are gitignored. This file
is the bibliography. `papers_review.xlsx` is the reading/notes tracker.

Titles, authors and DOIs were extracted from the PDFs; a few truncated author
lists are marked "et al." and one item could not be reliably identified.

- Liu, H. (2010). *Dependency direction as a means of word-order typology: A method based on dependency treebanks.* Lingua 120(10), 1567–1578. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.lingua.2009.10.001
- Gregory, M. L., & Michaelis, L. A. (2001). *Topicalization and Left-Dislocation: A Functional Opposition Revisited.* Journal of Pragmatics 33, 1665–1706.
- Smolka, V. (2011). *Word-Order Variability and FSP in Written and Spoken Discourse.* https://doi.org/10.2478/v10017-011-0002-1
- Sankaravelayuthan, R. (2020). *Word Order Typology and Language Universals.* Preprint. https://doi.org/10.13140/RG.2.2.19122.22728
- *Given/new information* — `papers/givennew.pdf`: title/author not reliably extractable from the file (the first page is a reference list); likely Prince, E. F. (1981), *Toward a Taxonomy of Given–New Information* — unverified.
